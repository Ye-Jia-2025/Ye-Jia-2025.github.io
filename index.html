Biography

Ye Jia, IEEE Member, is a professor and doctoral supervisor at the School of Electrical Engineering, Chongqing University. His research focuses on wireless communication system design, analysis, and optimization, as well as the intersection of communications and power systems. He has published over 40 research papers in related fields.

In recent years, he has led projects such as the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) Young Scientists Fund and the Chongqing Natural Science Foundation Innovation and Development Joint Fund. He has also participated in projects such as the National Key R&D Program and the National Natural Science Foundation of China General Program. In 2022, he received the IEEE Andrea Goldsmith Young Scholars Award.

He is currently an Associate Editor for IEEE Communications Letters. He is a recipient of the National Excellent Young Scholars Fund (Overseas), the National Natural Science Foundation of China Excellent Young Scholars Fund (Overseas), and the Chongqing Talents-Young Top Talents Fund.

Research Directions

Dr. Ye Jia's main research area is wireless transmission technology and its applications, including non-terrestrial networks, smart reflectors, multi-antenna transmission systems, artificial intelligence technology, etc. His research focuses on communication systems, power systems, and the combination of communication and power.

He has published over 40 research papers in renowned international and domestic journals, including IEEE Wireless Communications, IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications, IEEE Transactions on Communications, and IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology. He also participated in the publication of an English monograph by Springer Nature.
